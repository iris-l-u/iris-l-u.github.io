Green Tea

Green tea is prepared from the fresh, or withered, lightly heated/steamed leaves of the Camellia Stinensis plant. Unlike Black tea, this preparation stops the oxidation process and accounts for Green tea’s flavor profile — one that’s light, fresh, and maybe a little bit grassy.

Many different Green teas may have slightly different flavors that can range from nutty, to fruity, to seaweed-like (ex: matcha, sencha, hojicha). These flavors are usually dependent on where the tea was grown, as well as how it was processed.
